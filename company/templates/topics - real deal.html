Hyperconverged Infrastructure (HCI)

From the Company...

    Hyperconverged infrastructure (HCI) is a software-defined, unified system that combines all the elements of a traditional data center: storage, compute, networking, and management. This integrated solution uses software and x86 servers to replace expensive, purpose-built hardware. With hyperconverged infrastructure, you’ll decrease data center complexity and increase scalability.

What That Acutall Means:

    This product line is focused on:
    1) Giving companies a single package to run and operate their data centers

Why it exsists:

    Traditionally, when a company determines configuration options for their data center they have to determine four major caterogies:
    1) What machines they want to use to store the data (storage)
    2) The resourcing needs the storage machines need to have in order to meet business goals (compute)
    3) How the data is going to be transfered between the machines and software applications (networking)
    4) How the IT team is going to manage all of the above pieces (management)
    HCI simplifies those decisions by offering a solution that combines each of the four decisions above. HCI is a software defined data center meaning that companies can easily and cost effectively scale their growth for future needs.
